Frequently Asked Questions

How does facial recognition work?

Facial recognition systems use artificial intelligence (AI) to analyze facial features and create a unique digital signature for each face. This signature can then be used to identify or verify a person's identity by comparing it to a database of known faces.

Will I be recognised with glasses / a beard / a new haircut?

Yes, most facial recognition systems will still be able to recognize you with glasses, a beard, or a new haircut. However, the accuracy of the system may be reduced if there are significant changes to your appearance.

How accurate is facial recognition?

The accuracy of facial recognition systems varies depending on a number of factors, including the quality of the image, the lighting conditions, and the size and diversity of the database being used. However, in ideal conditions, facial recognition systems can be extremely accurate.

Is facial recognition biased?

Yes, facial recognition systems can be biased, depending on the data they are trained on and the algorithms used. For example, studies have shown that facial recognition systems are less accurate at identifying people of color and women than they are at identifying white men.
